The European School of Metabolomics 2026 has wrapped up, and it was a memorable week. More than seventy early career scientists travelled to Leiden from across Europe and beyond for a week full of metabolomics, hands-on workshops, and plenty of good conversations between sessions. From our group, the school was experienced from three different angles — as instructor, as organizer, and as student — and together they say a lot about what makes this event worth the effort.

*As an instructor.* Justin taught across several sessions, covering the path from raw LC-MS(/MS) data to the samples and features genuinely worth investigating, and closing with a lecture on machine learning and annotation. A demo of the prioritization tool *FERMO* helped tie together many of the concepts introduced earlier in the course. There was also room for a bit of experimentation in the teaching itself: an impromptu round of bingo in the middle of an experimental design lecture. Unconventional, certainly, but it turned out to be a surprisingly effective way to keep everyone engaged.

*Behind the scenes.* Larissa was part of the organizing committee and the scientific board, helping shape the program and keep the whole week running smoothly. She also stepped onto the teaching side during the hands-on mzmine session, assisting Yann Guitton. Organizing a school on this scale takes real energy and coordination, but seeing it all come together — a full week of good science, good food, and good people — makes it well worth it.

*In the room as a student.* Paula joined EUSM2026 as a participant, taking part in the workshops, lectures, and social activities that give the school much of its character. And that's a big part of the point: the conversations between sessions, the shared meals, and the networks that form and tend to last well beyond the week itself — the human ones, not just the molecular ones.

![EUSM 2026](/images/news/EUSM_collage.png)

Three different seats in the same room, and the same conclusion: a week that was as enjoyable as it was intense, and one that left everyone proud to have been part of it. The next EUSM will take place in two years' time. Where it will be held is still anyone's guess, but we very much hope to be there again.
