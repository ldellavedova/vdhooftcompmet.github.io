Joana Tirana received her Electrical and Computer Engineering degree (MSc-equivalent) from the University of Thessaly in Volos, Greece, in 2021. Her MSc thesis explored drone-based parallel task execution across edge nodes. She completed a software engineering internship at 3DEXCITE (Dassault Systèmes) in Munich, where she developed an interest in distributed and operating systems. 

Then, she pursued her PhD in computer science at University College Dublin (2022 - 2025), focusing on distributed and collaborative machine learning, where the users' data remain decentralized for privacy reasons. Her research addressed resource-constrained environments, where users operate on low-capacity devices, and investigated Split Federated Learning (SFL) as a solution. Her work combined system design and theoretical analysis, including the development of open-source frameworks and mathematical models describing the training workflow.

During her PhD, she was a research visitor at TU Delft and an intern researcher at Telefónica Innovación Digital in Barcelona, contributing to projects aligned with her doctoral research.

In 2026, she joined the Bioinformatics group at Wageningen University & Research as a postdoctoral researcher under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Marnix Medema, Dr. Justin van der Hooft, and Dr. Daniel Probst. Her work contributes to the KIC PRIORITY project, which aims to explore novel genomic and chemical space for antimicrobial discovery using federated learning, enabling collaborative model training across institutions without sharing sensitive data.

Joana’s main research focus is on developing and optimizing machine learning systems, training models using distributed machine learning algorithms, and deep neural networks. Outside of work, she enjoys cycling, going to the gym, and watching movies at old vintage cinemas.
